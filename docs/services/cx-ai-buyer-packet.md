## What Is Included

The sprint covers one production-relevant workflow and delivers:

- workflow map across user, AI agent, tools, policies, context, and reviewer,
- risk model for policy freshness, PII/context, source grounding, tool
  actions, escalation, eval, observability, or security boundaries,
- 10-14 replay tests or test specifications,
- reliability scorecard for acceptance and regression checks,
- 48h / 2w / 6w implementation path,
- optional bounded prototype or fixture when access is available and the
  change is narrow.

## Payment Routes

Payment can be handled after scope confirmation by Wise payment request,
Stripe invoice, PayPal invoice, bank transfer, or USDC.

Payment links or invoices are issued only after the buyer confirms:

- billing legal name or company,
- billing email,
- billing address if required by procurement,
- preferred payment method,
- selected workflow,
- reviewer / acceptance owner,
- preferred kickoff date.

## Data Handling

The default sprint does not require production credentials, raw payment data,
regulated health records, private keys, or full customer exports.

Useful inputs include approved policies, sanitized workflow notes, anonymized
transcripts, screenshots, current eval examples, reviewer judgment, public
docs, and bounded repo areas when prototype work is included.

Do not send secrets or private customer data unless a separate written
agreement and transfer method are approved first.

## Acceptance

The sprint is accepted when the selected workflow has:

- a written workflow map,
- a policy, PII/context, source-grounding, action, escalation, eval,
  observability, or security risk model,
- 10-14 replay tests or test specifications,
- a scorecard the reviewer can apply,
- a concrete implementation path with owners, sequencing, and known gaps.

The delivery payment is tied to accepted delivery of those artifacts for the
selected workflow.

## First 48 Hours

After kickoff payment and access are in place, the buyer receives:

- workflow and boundary confirmation,
- first risk hypotheses,
- replay-test outline,
- data or access gaps,
- acceptance checklist for the final handoff.

This checkpoint is meant to catch scope drift early and keep the engagement
focused on one workflow.

## Procurement FAQ

**Is this legal, compliance, medical, or financial certification?**  
No. The sprint produces engineering reliability evidence and implementation
guidance for one selected workflow. Formal certification is out of scope.

**Can the work start without production credentials?**  
Yes. The default engagement starts from sanitized docs, screenshots,
anonymized traces, public docs, current evals, or reviewer-provided examples.

**Can a prototype be included?**  
Yes, when access is available and the change is narrow. The core deliverable
remains the replay suite, scorecard, risk model, and implementation path.

**Can the sprint cover multiple products or workflows?**  
Not in the default scope. A second workflow needs a separate scope decision.

**What happens if USD 18,000 cannot be approved immediately?**  
Use the USD 3,000 diagnostic fallback to produce a workflow map, top five
failure modes, five replay tests, and a full-sprint recommendation.
